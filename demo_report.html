Methodology

Paired-end FASTQ files (R1 + R2) were processed through FastP for adapter trimming, quality filtering, and paired-end merging. Merged reads were aligned to the reference peptide library using Diamond BLASTX, yielding per-read amino acid and position assignments. Read counts were aggregated per amino acid per position per sample.

Two complementary analyses are performed: (1) Within-condition — an intercept-only binomial GLM is fit at each position–AA pair; the log-odds coefficient reflects how frequently that amino acid is observed, shown in the sequence logo. (2) Pairwise — a full likelihood-ratio test (LRT) compares a condition-coded model against the intercept-only model. The slope coefficient gives the log-odds ratio between conditions; positive = enriched in the test condition. All p-values are Benjamini–Hochberg corrected.

Key Findings
  • 1 8 significant enrichments identified (FDR < 5%) across 4 positions (P3, P6, P8, P11), out of 12 positions tested across 20 amino acids.
  • 2 Position 6 shows the strongest signal (LRT = 38.1, padj = 0.0001): glutamic acid (E) is replaced by lysine (K) or arginine (R), a dramatic acidic → basic charge reversal, suggesting electrostatic optimisation of the epitope–receptor interface.
  • 3 Position 3 shows tyrosine (Y) → tryptophan (W) enrichment (LRT = 24.7, padj = 0.00024). Both residues are aromatic polar, but tryptophan's larger indole ring may provide additional hydrophobic surface burial or extended π-stacking interactions.
  • 4 Position 8 shows K → R enrichment (LRT = 19.5, padj = 0.00082). Both residues are basic; arginine's guanidinium group offers additional hydrogen-bond donors and is commonly enriched at binding interfaces.
  • 5 Position 11 shows D → N enrichment (LRT = 17.2, padj = 0.00142): removal of the aspartate negative charge. Taken together with positions 6 and 8, the overall selection pressure favours a net positive charge increase across the peptide, consistent with binding to an acidic receptor surface.
